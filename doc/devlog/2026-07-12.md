# Devlog - 2026-07-12

## Water Shader Rework

Rewrote the sea and river shaders. Previously, the ocean and rivers used
separate water treatments, so color, reflection, and wave motion visibly broke
at their boundaries. The new pass puts both surfaces on the same visual basis,
so they read as one connected water system.

![River water around a wooden bridge using the new shader, with splash effects around bridge posts and rocks.](<images/water-shader-bridge-splashes.png>)

The biggest change is the seamless sea-river transition. Where a river flows
into the sea, the surface no longer snaps between two unrelated looks; only the
intended shallow/deep water variation remains.

![A coastline and river mouth where shallow water, river water, and ocean water blend into a continuous surface.](<images/water-shader-coast-transition.png>)

Ocean wave motion was also improved. Large distant swells and smaller nearby
ripples now blend more consistently, keeping broad water surfaces from reading
like a flat scrolling texture.

Rivers now react to obstacles as well. Rocks, bridge posts, and other objects
that interrupt the flow spawn splash effects around them, making those points
look like places where water is actually colliding with geometry instead of
static decoration.
